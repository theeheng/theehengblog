Hogart review September

William Hogart

Like the Barbara Hepworth show of few years earlier, William Hogarth was held in the main wing, there being no dedicated exhibition gallery at that time. However, instead of white modernist alcoves for sculptures, the temporary space created was a pastiche of an 18th century upper class home, complete with flock wallpaper and dado rails

The exhibition was based on recent scolarship and aimed to confirm Hogart's reputation as an important artist. Tate director Norman Reid noted in the exhibition catalogue that although Hogart was one of the great painter he was a 'rather awkward figure to cope with in the context of art as a whole... His appeal to an audience far wider than the normal art-loving public has perhaps made him slightly suspect ot the connoisseur and aesthete'.
